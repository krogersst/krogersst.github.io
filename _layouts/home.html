naturalist

I'm a STEAM-focused naturalist with experience at multiple institutions across the state of Ohio.

STEAM-Focused Programming

STEAM (Science Technology Engineering Art and Math) programming and environmental education belong together. From preschoolers experimenting with simple tools to explore bird beak or turtle feet adaptations to engineering winter homes for native Ohio animals, STEAM and nature go hand in hand.

Check It Out

Build a Winter Home Engineering Drop-By Activity

Using natural materials such as moss, leaves, sticks, grasses and more, participants build winter homes or hibernacula for critters to stay warm in the winter.

Related books were also made available for an optional reading activity for solo or partnered reading. During this drop-by, a father read Bear Snores On with his youngest after they made their own home, while the older siblings made multiple iterations of winter homes.

Ohio and Beyond

Between my time at Aullwood Audubon Nature Center and Farm, Columbus Zoo and Aquarium, Great Lakes Science Center and Cleveland Metroparks, I've helped guests overcome their fear of birds by helping them hand feed lorikeets, provided care for a one-eyed screech owl, and introduced toddlers and senior citizens alike to the wonders of Ohio mushrooms.

If you're looking for urban wildlife walks, mammoths hiding in suburbia, or mushroom hikes in the woods, I'd love to go exploring with you!

librarian

I'm a trained librarian with a passion for digital cultural and natural heritage projects.

Training

In May 2020, I graduated with my Master of Library and Information Science from Kent State University with a focus in digital cultural and natural heritage.

My studies ranged from local history and genealogy database user research, zoos and nature centers within a museums and information science context, and digital preservation workflows that are viable for small institutions with limited resources.

Read More

Local History and Genealogy

My digitization and archival skills were honed in local history collections and collections with limited resources, focusing on photography and ephemera. These include Kenton County Public Library Local History and Genealogy, Cleveland State University Special Collections, International Women's Air and Space Museum, and most recently Bay Village Historical Society's Rose Hill Museum.

I also have extensive experience with genealogical research and leading one-on-one research sessions with library patrons to help find their family history. I enjoy teaching others how to combine information from the big names like Ancestry, Fold3, and FamilySearch with more obscure databases at the city and county level.
